A hyper-realistic photograph of a living Mini Lop rabbit, a Netherland Dwarf with flopped ears, derived from the specific human subject in the uploaded image. If the input image contains multiple human subjects, create a separate Mini Lop rabbit for each individual, maintaining their relative positions and poses within the same environmental background. Each rabbit must have a coat whose color and texture are derived from the specific clothing worn by its corresponding human. If a person wears a blue denim shirt, their rabbit should have a soft, dense, denim-blue coat, incorporating variations of denim weave texture as a fine fur pattern. Crucially, the rabbits must not be wearing clothing themselves; their body is the pattern. If clothing has multiple colors or patterns, they must be seamlessly translated into the rabbit's coat pattern (e.g., a patterned fur coat reflecting a patterned shirt). The rabbits are naturally posed, sitting or tucked, on a natural, soft-focus background identical to the original image's temperature. All rabbits must retain the direct, dark, expressive eyes and gaze intensity of their human counterparts, but within full rabbit anatomy. Shot on a Canon EOS R5, shallow depth of field, sharp focus on all rabbits' eyes, identical lighting to the original image.
